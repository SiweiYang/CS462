Axel Thue

Axel Thue was born in Tönsberg, Norway, on February 19, 1863. In 1889 he received a degree at the University of Oslo. From 1903 until 1922 he was professor in applied mechanics at the University of Oslo. He was elected to the Norweigian Academy of Science and Letters in 1894, and he was associate editor of Acta Mathematica from 1916-1922.

Thue is sometimes known as the "father of formal languages". He was the first to investigate the problem of finding an infinite sequence over a finite alphabet that did not contain any occurrences of adjacent identical blocks, a property today known as squarefreeness. Unfortunately, because his papers on repetitions in words were published in a relatively obscure Scandinavian journal, his work was forgotten and later rediscovered by many writers, such as Max Euwe and Marston Morse. However, Gustav Hedlund later rediscovered Thue's contribution and popularized it.

Thue also made fundamental contributions to number theory (particularly transcendence theory).

Thue died in Oslo, Norway, on March 7, 1922.
